## PART 1 — Internal Linking Map

### The principle
Your **home** and **case study** pages are the pages you want to rank and convert. Everything else (blog, about) should pass authority *toward* them. Blog posts attract search traffic on topics; internal links then funnel that authority and those visitors to your money pages.

Three rules:
1. **No orphan pages** — every page must be linked from at least one other page.
2. **Descriptive anchor text** — the clickable words should describe the destination (a ranking signal). Never "click here."
3. **3-click rule** — every important page reachable within 3 clicks of the home page (solved by a clear nav + footer).

### Site-wide links (nav + footer — every page)
| Element | Links to | Anchor text |
|---|---|---|
| Top nav | `/work` | Work |
| Top nav | `/about` | About |
| Top nav | `/blog` | Blog |
| Top nav | `/contact` | Contact |
| Footer | `/` | Neha — Experience & Product Designer |
| Footer | social profiles | Behance, Dribbble, LinkedIn, etc. (external) |

### Page-by-page link map
| Source page | Links to | Recommended anchor text |
|---|---|---|
| Home `/` | `/work` | View my design work |
| Home `/` | best 2–3 case studies | [Project name] — [what it was] |
| Home `/` | `/blog` | Read my writing on UX |
| Home `/` | `/contact` | Get in touch |
| About `/about` | `/work` | See selected case studies |
| About `/about` | `/contact` | Work with me |
| Case study `/work/x` | 1–2 *other* case studies | related project by type/industry |
| Case study `/work/x` | `/contact` | Start a project like this |
| Case study `/work/x` | a relevant blog post | how I approach [topic] |
| Blog post | `/` or `/about` | "experience designer" / "product designer" → home |
| Blog post | relevant case study | real example: [project] |
| Blog post | 1–2 *other* blog posts | related read on [topic] |
| Blog `/blog` (index) | each post | post title |

### Apply this to the UX post you just wrote
From `/blog/user-experience-design`, add these internal links:
- Anchor **"experience designer"** (in the intro or author line) → `/` (home)
- Anchor **"View my work"** (author bio, already there) → `/work`
- Anchor **"get in touch"** (author bio, already there) → `/contact`
- When you publish a UX case study, add: **"See this in practice: [project]"** → that case study
- When you write a second post (e.g. "UX vs UI"), cross-link both ways

### Topic clusters (the AI-search multiplier)
Group related posts around one "pillar" page so Google and AI models see you as a UX authority:

```
PILLAR:  /blog/user-experience-design   (broad, your current post)
  ├─ /blog/ux-vs-ui-design
  ├─ /blog/ux-design-process
  ├─ /blog/ux-in-healthcare        (you already have great material)
  └─ /blog/what-makes-good-ux
```
Every cluster post links up to the pillar; the pillar links down to each. This structure is one of the strongest signals for both SEO and GEO (AI answers).

## PART 2 — Outreach & Backlink Emails

Keep every pitch short, personalized (reference something specific), and lead with value to *them*, not a request. Send from neha@zoop.one or a designwithneha.in address. Follow up once after ~5–7 days.

### A. Guest post pitch (design publications)
> **Subject:** Guest article idea: [specific angle] for [Publication]
>
> Hi [Editor name],
>
> I'm Neha, an experience & product designer. I've been reading [Publication] for a while — your recent piece on [specific article] resonated, especially [one specific point].
>
> I'd love to contribute an original article. A few angles I could write from real project experience:
> 1. [Specific, non-generic title]
> 2. [Specific title]
> 3. [Specific title]
>
> Each would be ~1,200–1,500 words, original, with practical examples. Happy to send an outline first.
>
> A bit about me: [1 line — years, notable work, or a niche like healthcare UX]. Portfolio: designwithneha.in
>
> Would any of these be a fit?
>
> Thanks,
> Neha

>
> Love what you're doing with [Podcast] — the episode with [guest] on [topic] stuck with me.
>

>
> Thanks,

### D. Expert-quote / HARO-style response
*(Use on Featured, Qwoted, Help a B2B Writer. Answer fast, be quotable, keep it tight.)*
> Hi [Journalist name],
>
> Responding to your request on [topic].
>
> **Quote:** "[1–3 punchy, quotable sentences that directly answer their question. Make it specific and opinionated so it's worth quoting.]"
>
> **Attribution:** Neha, Experience & Product Designer, designwithneha.in
> **Bio (1 line):** [years + focus area].
>
> Happy to expand if useful.
> Neha

### E. Republish-with-canonical note (Medium / Hashnode / dev.to)
When reposting your own blog to Medium etc., add this line at the bottom and set the canonical URL to your original:
> *Originally published on [designwithneha.in/blog/user-experience-design](https://designwithneha.in/blog/user-experience-design).*

This drives traffic and a link back without duplicate-content issues.
